Utility and Beauty

I get caught in a fight between the utility of the Web and the beauty of it. I want to make things beautiful and hand crafted, but we always have to remember the limitations of the current wave of mobile devices.

Art direction for the Web is a real thing with the advent of CSS Grid and Flexbox. We now have strong control of the way elements can be laid out on the Web. However, I sometimes get disheartened by the need for utility on the Web because of how many people are accessing our sites and apps via current mobile devices. We have all the ability in the world to create Ferarris but everyone is driving a Smart Car.

What helps me to continue pushing forward with these ideas is that the mobile future is wearable. One day we'll all put our smart phones down and put on a pair of glasses that overlays the world with all of the beautiful things we can create. Reality will probably be much different this vision, but I can dream, right?

When this future comes, we'll be able to art direct all of the digital artifacts—beautiful virtual magazines and newspapers!—that we create and people will actually be able to notice the beauty of the Web over its utility. I'm thankful for where we are right now, but I'm tired of the internal tug of war. I'm ready for awesome layouts to mean something. I'm tired of stacking boxes.

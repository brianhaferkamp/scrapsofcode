Ghosted by Clients

In the past month I've been ghosted by two clients.

I guess I don't mind it so much. It's a part of dealing with lower end clients. Well, freelance projects, in general.

The thing that bothers me about it is that I don't get a chance to talk through the objections. They don't even say, "Hey! That's way too much money. How can you charge that??" I could at least answer that and get into a money talk with them.

I'm not looking for a lot. Just a "no, thanks" or "I saw your proposal and it's not a good fit." Something like that.

I watch a lot of videos from The Futur on YouTube. They share content for creative professionals on handling clients and more of the business/psychology side of being a creative in business. (Here's a particularly good stream on pricing creative work.)

They suggest having the money talk very early on. From my experiences above, it seems like the best course of action. Between the lead call and proposal I spend a couple of hours that don't get recouped at all. At least a critique of my process or proposal would be worth the time I put into it. It could help me get better.

If you're a freelancer, have you had similar experiences with clients? Is it something that is getting more or less prevalent? If you've been able to find a way around it, please share.
